Here are a few layouts for automating items that you will always need. It is good to get these set up a soon as you can so that the resources can start going into storage for later use.

Any Input and Output values are measured in items per minute, as that is what the game uses to calculate amounts of items, ratios, and belt speeds as well. If there is no clock speed listed with the placement of a building, then it should be at 100% clockspeed. Pay attention to what level converyer belts you have unlocked, and make sure that you can transport the required number of materials on them.

Reinforced Iron Plates

Input: 60 Iron Ore
Output: 5 Reinforced Iron Plates

Phase 1: 60 iron ore go into 2 smelters to produce 60 iron ingots. Merge the outputs, then set up a splitter system as shown in the image to split the belt into 2 belts - one will have 15 iron ingots and the other 45.

Phase 2: Send the belt of 45 into 2 constructors at 75% clock speed producing iron plates. Send the other belt of 15 iron ingots into one constructor producing 15 iron rods. Send those iron rods into 2 constructors at 75% clock speed producing screws. Lastly, send the belt of screws and iron plates into an assembler producing 5 reinforced iron plates.

Rotors

Input: 45 Iron Ore
Output: 4 Rotors

Phase 1: 45 iron ore go into 2 smelters at 75% clock speed producing iron ingots. Those iron ingots will then be split into 3 constructors to produce 45 iron rods total.

Phase 2: Split 25 of them off (an overflow system will work fine here) into 3 constructors at 83.333% clock speed producing 100 screws. Lastly, send the 20 iron rods and 100 screws into 1 assembler producing 4 rotors.

Modular Frames

Input: 48 Iron ore
Output: 2 Modular Frames

Phase 1: 48 iron ore go into 2 smelters at 80% clock speed producing iron ingots. 27 Iron ingots go to one constructor producing 18 iron plates at 90% clock speed. The other 21 iron ingots go into 2 contructors producing 21 iron rods at 70% clock speed.

Phase 2: Send 9 rods to 1 constructor producing 36 screws at 90% clock speed. Send all screws an iron plates into one assembler producing 3 reinforced iron plates at 60% clock speed. Lastly, combine the reinforced iron plates and the remaining 12 rods from before into one assembler producing 2 modular frames.
